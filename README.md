# Lila Studio --- User Guide

### Content Generation, Review, and Scheduling

This guide walks through the full content workflow inside Lila Studio:
generating AI video and images, reviewing and approving content, and
scheduling it for posting.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1. Content Generation

Content Generation is where every piece of content starts. The page has
two modes, switchable from the top tabs: **Video Generation** and
**Image Generation**.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120000.png)

### 1.1 Video Generation

Video generation uses the **Wan2.2 I2V-A14B (high, fp8 scaled)** model
--- an image-to-video model that animates a single reference image into
a multi-scene video.

#### Step 1 --- Upload a Reference Image

Every video starts from a reference image. This image anchors the
character/subject's appearance across every scene of the video.

-   Drag and drop an image onto the upload area, or click to browse
-   Accepted formats: PNG, JPG, WEBP (up to 20MB)
-   Once uploaded, you can preview it, replace it, or remove it

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120104.png)

**Tip --- getting a stable, clean result:** For the most reliable
output, keep each scene around **10 seconds**, which at 16 FPS
corresponds to about **81 frames per scene** by default. If you want
longer or smoother scenes, you can increase **Frames Per Scene**, but
you should raise **Sampling Steps** alongside it --- more frames need
more sampling steps to stay stable and avoid flicker or warping. As a
rule of thumb: the longer your scene, the higher your steps should go.

#### Step 3 --- Build Your Scenes

A video is made of multiple scenes, generated individually and stitched
together into one final clip. The Scene Builder lets you write a
separate prompt for each scene.

-   **Add Scene** --- adds a new scene slot to the end of the sequence
-   **Reorder** --- use the up/down arrows on each scene card to change
    its position in the final video
-   **Remove** --- delete a scene (at least one scene must remain)
-   **Prompt field** --- describe the camera framing, the action,
    lighting, and mood for that specific scene (e.g. *"A stable
    medium-wide shot. She reaches down and opens a notebook, sketching a
    butterfly. The lines begin to glow with golden light."*)

The header shows a live counter of how many scenes are fully written out
(e.g. "8 / 10 configured"), so you can track progress before generating.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120146.png)
\#### Step 4 --- Negative Prompt

The negative prompt tells the model what to avoid across the whole video
--- things like blurry motion, watermark, distorted hands, sudden cuts,
or static/frozen frames. A sensible default is pre-filled, but you can
edit it freely.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120156.png)

#### Step 5 --- Review the Summary and Generate

On the right-hand side, the **Generation Summary** panel gives you a
live, read-only snapshot of your job before you submit it:

-   Reference image thumbnail
-   Total scenes, FPS, frames per scene, sampling steps
-   Total frames and estimated final video duration
-   Estimated generation time

When everything is filled in (reference image uploaded + every scene
prompt written), the **Generate Video** button becomes active.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120246.png)

#### Step 6 --- Watch It Generate

Once submitted, a progress card appears showing:

-   A live progress bar and percentage
-   A step-by-step checklist (job submitted → workers initialising →
    loading models → generating each scene batch → stitching final video
    → uploading → complete)
-   Elapsed time
-   A **Stop** button to cancel the job at any point

You can navigate away from the page and come back --- the job continues
running and the progress picks back up automatically.


#### Step 7 --- Get Your Result

When generation finishes, a result card appears with:

-   An embedded video player to preview the final clip
-   **Download** button (saves as .mp4)
-   **Copy URL** button
-   **Open** button (opens the video in a new tab)
-   If the video was generated in multiple batches, a "batch chunks"
    expandable list lets you view each individual segment
-   The finished video is automatically added to your library, ready to
    be picked up in the Review Queue


If generation fails for any reason, an error card explains what went
wrong and gives you a **Dismiss and retry** option.

### 1.2 Image Generation

Image generation uses the **Qwen Image Edit** model, which edits or
generates a new image based on a reference image plus a text prompt ---
useful for placing a character into a new scene, changing lighting/mood,
or applying stylistic edits.

#### Step 1 --- Upload a Reference Image

Same as video --- upload the base image that will be edited or used as
the generation anchor.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120308.png)

#### Step 2 --- Write Your Prompt

Describe exactly what you want changed or generated, e.g. *"A futuristic
city with a slightly dark neon atmosphere and glowing street lights. The
girl in the foreground, her face and body well lit by the street
lighting."*

Two additional controls sit alongside the prompt:

-   **Size** --- choose the output resolution/aspect ratio: 512×512,
    768×768, 1024×1024, 1024×768 (landscape), or 768×1024 (portrait)
-   **Seed** --- set a specific seed number for reproducible results, or
    click **Random** to let the model pick a new seed each time

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120402.png)

#### Step 3 --- Review the Summary and Generate

The **Image Summary** panel on the right shows the reference thumbnail,
model name, chosen size, seed, output format (JPEG), and confirms
auto-save to your library. Click **Generate Image** once a reference
image and prompt are both set.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120445.png)

#### Step 4 --- Watch It Generate

A compact progress card shows elapsed time and a Queued/Running badge,
with a **Stop** button if you need to cancel.

`[SCREENSHOT: Generating Image card — progress bar, timer, Stop button]`

#### Step 5 --- Get Your Result

The finished image appears full-size with:

-   **Download** (.jpg)
-   **Copy URL**
-   **Open** in a new tab
-   Automatic save to your content library

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120508.png)

## 2. Review Queue

Every piece of generated content --- image or video --- lands
automatically in the **Review Queue**, where it waits for a decision:
approve, reject, or send back for changes.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120544.png)

### 2.1 Browsing the Queue

At the top of the page, summary cards show how many items are
**Pending**, **Approved**, **Scheduled**, and **Rejected** at a glance.

Below that, you can:

-   **Search** by character name, caption, or job ID
-   **Filter by status** (Pending, Approved, Rejected, Scheduled,
    Published)
-   **Filter by type** (Video or Image)

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120544.png)

Each content card in the grid shows:

-   A thumbnail (videos preview on hover)
-   The content type (image/video) and current status badge
-   The post type badge (**Normal** or **PPV**, with price if PPV)
-   Character name and current caption
-   Quick **Approve** / **Reject** buttons --- or, once approved, a
    **Schedule** button appears in their place

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120638.png)

You can also select multiple cards using the checkbox in the corner of
each thumbnail, which opens a bulk action bar to **Approve selected** or
**Reject selected** all at once.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120544.png)
![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120654.png)

### 2.2 The Detail View

Clicking on any card opens a full detail panel with everything you need
to make a decision.

**Media preview** --- full-size image or video player at the top, so you
can review the actual output before deciding.

**Approve / Reject / Schedule now** --- depending on the current
status: - A pending item shows **Approve** and **Reject** - An approved
item shows **Schedule now**, which jumps straight into scheduling that
piece of content

**Post Details** --- this is where you shape how the content will
actually be posted:

-   **Post Type** --- toggle between **Normal Post** and **PPV
    (Pay-Per-View) Post**
-   **Price** --- if PPV is selected, choose a quick preset (\$5 / \$15
    / \$20) or type a custom price
-   **Caption** --- write the caption that will accompany the post

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120813.png)

Click **Save post details in review queue** to lock in your changes. If
you click **Schedule now** without saving first, your most recent edits
are still used --- nothing is lost.

**Reviewer notes** --- a free-text field for internal notes
(e.g. "re-render scene 3, lighting is off"), separate from the public
caption. Useful for tracking feedback on content that needs
regeneration.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120654.png)

**Review history** --- a timeline showing every step the content has
gone through: generated → queued for review → approved/rejected →
scheduled, each with a timestamp.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120923.png)

## 3. Scheduling

Once content is approved, it moves to the **Scheduling** page, where you
plan exactly when it gets posted.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120800.png)

### 3.1 Connecting Your Posting Account

Before anything can be published, connect your posting account from the
**Connect Account** button in the top right. Once connected, you'll see
your account name and a green "connected" indicator at all times.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20123131.png)

### 3.2 Scheduling Content

Click **Schedule content** to open the scheduling dialog:

1.  Pick an **approved** asset from the dropdown (only approved content
    shows up here)
2.  Review its post details (post type, price, caption) --- pulled
    directly from what you set in the Review Queue
3.  Confirm your connected posting account
4.  Pick a **date** and **time**
5.  Click **Schedule**

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120813.png)

### 3.3 Calendar View

The Calendar view lays out the week ahead, day by day. Each scheduled
post appears as a small card under its publish day, showing the
thumbnail, content type icon, status badge, character, caption,
scheduled time, and post type badge.

-   Use the arrows to move between weeks, or **Today** to jump back to
    the current week
-   **Drag and drop** any card onto a different day to reschedule it
    instantly

`[SCREENSHOT: Calendar view — full week grid with scheduled post cards on various days]`

### 3.4 Publishing Queue

The Queue view lists everything that's scheduled, in the process of
publishing, or has failed --- in a simple list rather than a calendar.

Each row shows the thumbnail, character, caption, post type, scheduled
time, and queue status. From here you can:

-   **Publish now** --- push the post live immediately, ahead of its
    scheduled time
-   **Retry** --- for any failed post, requeue it
-   Open the **detail view** or **cancel** the scheduled post entirely
    via the menu

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120857.png)

Content that's overdue (past its scheduled time) is published
automatically in the background, so you don't have to babysit the queue
--- though you can always step in manually with **Publish now**.

### 3.5 History

The History tab is your full record of everything that's been published
or failed to publish --- with thumbnail, post type, publish date, a link
to the live post, and final status.

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120911.png)

### 3.6 Scheduled Item Detail View

Clicking any scheduled or published item opens a detail sheet showing:

-   The media preview
-   Full **Post Details** (post type, price, caption) --- exactly what
    was/will be sent live
-   Scheduled time and connected posting account
-   A direct link to the live post once published
-   Full history timeline
-   Action buttons: **Publish now**, **Retry** (if failed), or
    **Remove** from the schedule

![Generation page tab switcher](Screenshot%202026-06-30%20120923.png)

## 4. End-to-End Workflow Summary

1.  **Generate** --- Upload a reference image, write your prompt(s),
    tune your settings, and generate a video or image.
2.  **Review** --- The finished content lands in the Review Queue.
    Preview it, set the caption and post type/price, then Approve or
    Reject.
3.  **Schedule** --- Approved content moves to Scheduling, where you
    pick a date and time (or publish immediately) and it goes live to
    your connected account.
